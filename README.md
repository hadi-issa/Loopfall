## Loopfall

An original dementia-themed game inspired by *Everywhere at the End of Time* by The Caretaker.

---

## Overview
Loopfall is a physics-driven experience where the player controls a rolling ball navigating an unstable, deteriorating world. The game focuses on momentum, loss of control, and environmental decay to reflect the gradual breakdown of memory and perception.

Rather than relying on traditional storytelling, Loopfall communicates its themes through:
- physics behaviour
- environmental instability
- audio degradation
- repetition and looping structures

---

## Core Concept
The game is built around a simple idea:

> The world doesn’t just challenge you — it slowly stops making sense.

As the player progresses:
- surfaces become less reliable  
- movement becomes harder to control  
- familiar elements distort and degrade  
- the environment becomes increasingly unpredictable  

---

## Gameplay
- Physics-based movement using Unity Rigidbody  
- Momentum-driven traversal  
- Increasing environmental instability  
- Minimal UI and guidance  
- Progression through looping stages  

---

## Audio Design
Audio plays a central role in the experience.

The game uses ambient piano-based sound inspired by *Everywhere at the End of Time*, with progressive degradation:
- clean, structured audio early on  
- subtle distortion and instability  
- heavy noise, fragmentation, and loss of clarity  

The goal is to create recognition first, then discomfort.

---

## Themes
- Memory degradation  
- Loss of control  
- Repetition and decay  
- Disorientation  
- Emotional unease through systems, not narrative  

---

## Technical Details
- Engine: Unity  
- Movement: Rigidbody-based physics system  
- Focus: systemic design over scripted events
